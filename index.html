Jordan Robinson

I'm an Artificial Intelligence (AI) researcher that specialises in bespoke multi-agent Large Language Model framework development, Computational Models of Argument, Argumentation Theory, and Natural Language Processing. My research regards the design, development and testing of decision-support systems for anyone, such as intelligence analysts, investigators and businesses, who need to conduct defeasible reasoning under uncertainty. I recently finished writing my thesis and passed my viva voce with minor corrections. I'm now working at the University of Liverpool as a postdoc, researching the persuasiveness of Large Language Models in a number of settings.

Education

  • Computer Science, PhD - University of Liverpool (2020 - 2024)
    • My PhD project concerned the design, development and testing of AI tools, techniques and algorithms to provide decision- and reasoning-support to intelligence analysts, with a specific focus on aiding the all-source analyst when conducting hypothesis generation and evaluation. Summaries of each chapter are listed below:
      • A survey of the literature related to intelligence analysis, argumentation theory, computational models of argument, and AI tools for intelligence analyses.
      • A proposal for an argumentation scheme from multiple causes to effect to represent hypotheses that can be supported by a varying number of items of information, evidence, and assumptions.
      • A study which assessed the feasibility of leveraging large language models for hypothesis generation.
      • An algorithm for the refinement of intelligence analysts' prior probabilistic beliefs using the acceptability of arguments discovered through the semantic evaluation of probabilistic argumentation frameworks.
      • An algorithm for conducting sensitivity analysis on Dung's original abstract argumentation framework.
